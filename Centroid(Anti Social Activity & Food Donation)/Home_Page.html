ABOUT US



Aim

Our aim is to establish a connection between the citizens and the government organizations which are working towards development of the society. We plan to make our city the best place to live in where all the citizens live in peace and harmony. It is our sole aim to voice the problems faced by the citizens in their daily life and make sure that the problems are attended and solved as soon as possible.




Motivation

When we look at european countries we appreciate their living standards. The thought of people appreciating our country for the quality of life it provides motivates us to work and make our country even more beautiful then it already is.






Did You Know?

Our vision is to contribute in developing our nation and we are working towards it step by step starting with our city. In order for a country to progress, it requires the support of its citizens, it requires its citizens to be proud of the country's history and culture. History helps us become better informed citizens. People should be aware about their country's greatness and virtues and should always look up to their nation with utmost respect. So, here are some Historical & Cultural Facts about India that will surely ignite respect and patriotism for our country:    

DID YOU KNOW?

Haplogroup R1a-M420 which is a human Y-chromosome DNA haplogroup distributed in a large region in Eurasia, extending from Scandinavia and Central Europe to southern Siberia and South Asia originated in India.
DID YOU KNOW?

The Cholas were a Tamil Dynasty and their great empire included most of South-East Asia. They propagated Hinduism and Sanskrit in South-East Asia.
DID YOU KNOW?

Sanskrit is the mother of all the European languages. Sanskrit is the most suitable language for computer software, reported in Forbes magazine, July 1987.
DID YOU KNOW?

The art of Navigation was born in the river Sindh 6000 years ago. The very word Navigation is derived from the Sanskrit word NAVGATIH. The word navy is also derived from Sanskrit 'Nou'.
DID YOU KNOW?

The value of pi was first calculated by Budhayana, and he explained the concept of what is known as the Pythagorean Theorem. He discovered this in the 6th century long before the European mathematicians.
